About Amir

I am a physicist and research scientist, currently at the Department of Integrative Biology, and the Institute for Computational Engineering and Sciences at The University of Texas at Austin. I work and have worked on a wide of range of scientific questions and topics ranging from the subatomic world of high energy particles, to the microscopic world of biological macromolecules, to astrophysical phenomena happening on the grandest scales of the observable universe. My current research area of focus however, is and will be multiscale modelling of tumor growth for the upcoming years. Previously, I worked at the national Institute for Fusion Studies (IFS) and the Institute for Cellular & Molecular Biology at The University of Texas at Austin, as a PhD candidate in Physics, also in the field of Cosmology and Astrophysics under the directions of Dr. Robert Nemiroff -- NASA GSFC scientist and professor at Michigan Tech University -- where my research focus was primarily on the theoretical and computational aspects of Gamma Ray Bursts and their possible applications as Cosmological tools.
