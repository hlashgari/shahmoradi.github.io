More about Amir

Prior to joining ICES, I was very fortunate to work in multiple world-renown research institutes including the national Institute for Fusion Studies (IFS), the Institute for Cellular and Molecular Biology (iCMB), as well as the Center for Petroleum and Geosystems Engineering (CPGE) and the Center for Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, at The University of Texas at Austin, either during my PhD studies as a graduate research assistant or later on as research scientist. Prior to getting my PhD at UT Austin, I gained several years of research experience in theoretical and observational High Energy Astrophysics and Cosmology, under the directions of Dr. Robert Nemiroff -- NASA GSFC scientist and professor at Michigan Tech University -- where my research focus was primarily on the theoretical and computational aspects of Gamma Ray Bursts and their possible applications as Cosmological tools.
